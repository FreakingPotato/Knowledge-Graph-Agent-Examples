Three coordinated strategies for combining language models with DNA / RNA foundation models — discovered, scored, ranked, and evidence-linked.

Combining natural-language models with DNA / RNA foundation models opens a new design space: text grounds biological sequences with literature context, and sequences ground language models with measurable functional reality. We argue that progress in this multimodal direction will be driven by three coordinated strategies — (i) building strong unimodal genomic foundation models that scale long-context nucleotide sequence understanding, (ii) bridging text and sequence modalities through shared embeddings, instruction tuning, and text-conditioned generation, and (iii) deploying the resulting multimodal systems on downstream applications such as variant-effect prediction, regulatory annotation, and sequence-aware drug discovery.
