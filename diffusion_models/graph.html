Diffusion Models × Natural Language × Genomic Sequences — Knowledge Graph

Three coordinated developments at the intersection of diffusion-based generative modelling, natural-language tokens, and protein/DNA/RNA sequence design — discovered, scored, ranked, and evidence-linked.

Diffusion models began as a continuous-noise generative process for images but have rapidly extended to discrete tokens (text, DNA, RNA, protein sequences) and to high-dimensional structured biology. We argue that progress at this intersection will be driven by three coordinated developments: (i) continuous-time diffusion foundations (score-based modelling, classifier-free guidance, latent-space scaling) that supply the modelling vocabulary; (ii) discrete & language diffusion that adapts the framework to text and other token sequences and now reaches LLM scale; and (iii) biological sequence and structure diffusion that translates these advances into protein, DNA, and RNA design — closing the loop between natural-language conditioning and functional biomolecule generation.
